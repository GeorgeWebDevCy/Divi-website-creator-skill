The Process

7-Step Property Purchase Process

We guide you through every stage of your Cyprus property purchase with transparency and full legal protection at each milestone.

1
Property Selection & Reservation
Choose your property and sign a reservation agreement with deposit. We review and legally secure your reservation before you commit.
2
Legal Due Diligence
Title search at the Land Registry. We confirm ownership, check for mortgages, encumbrances, planning permits, and any legal issues.
3
Sale Agreement
We draft and negotiate your Sale & Purchase Agreement to protect your rights, specify completion dates, and set penalty clauses.
4
Payment & Taxes
Secure fund transfers. Full guidance on Stamp Duty, VAT, Transfer Fees, and all applicable taxes to avoid surprises.
5
Foreign Buyer Approval
For non-EU buyers: we manage the Council of Ministers application required for acquiring immovable property in Cyprus.
6
Title Deed Transfer
Transfer formally registered at the District Land Registry Office. Our team is present throughout to ensure a smooth handover.
7
Handover & Support
Legal possession transfer, utilities setup, communal agreements, and residency application support where applicable.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Why invest in real estate in Cyprus? +
Cyprus offers a stable, EU-regulated real estate market with strong capital appreciation, a transparent legal system based on English Common Law, 100% foreign ownership rights, and the potential to qualify for Permanent Residency with a qualifying investment of €300,000. The island's growing tourism, strategic location, and favourable tax environment make it one of the most attractive property markets in Southern Europe.
What are the legal steps to buying property in Cyprus? +
The key steps are: property selection and reservation, legal due diligence (title search, planning checks), drafting and signing the Sale & Purchase Agreement, stamp duty payment, obtaining Council of Ministers approval (for non-EU buyers), completing the transfer at the Land Registry, and receiving the title deed. We guide you through each stage to ensure full legal compliance and protection.
Can foreigners buy property in Cyprus? +
Yes. EU citizens may purchase property in Cyprus without restriction. Non-EU nationals can also buy property but must obtain Council of Ministers approval. In practice, approval is routinely granted for genuine residential purchases. Our firm manages the entire approval process on your behalf.
How can I get permanent residency through property? +
Non-EU nationals can qualify for Cyprus Permanent Residency by purchasing new residential property worth at least €300,000 (plus VAT) from a licensed developer, combined with proof of annual income from abroad of at least €50,000 (plus €15,000 per spouse and €10,000 per dependent child). We handle the complete residency application process alongside your property purchase.
Do I need a lawyer when buying property in Cyprus? +
While not legally mandatory, engaging a lawyer is strongly advised and is standard practice in Cyprus. A lawyer performs essential due diligence, ensures your contract protects your interests, handles stamp duty and land registry filings, manages the title deed transfer, and identifies any legal risks before you commit funds. The cost of a lawyer is minimal compared to the protection provided.
What taxes and fees are involved in a Cyprus property purchase? +
Buyers typically incur: Stamp Duty (0.15%–0.20% on the purchase price), VAT (19% on new builds from developers, or exempt on resale), Immovable Property Transfer Fees (3%–8% depending on value, may be reduced or waived on new builds), and legal fees. We provide a full cost breakdown before you sign anything.
What is a title deed transfer? +
A title deed (Certificate of Registration) is the official document confirming legal ownership of property in Cyprus. The transfer occurs at the District Land Registry Office and formally records the property in your name. Until a title deed is transferred, you hold contractual rights but not absolute legal title. We ensure the transfer is completed correctly and promptly.
How long does a property purchase take in Cyprus? +
A straightforward purchase typically takes 4–8 weeks from signing the Sale Agreement to completion. The Council of Ministers approval for non-EU buyers adds approximately 2–4 months. Title deed issuance on new developments may take longer depending on the developer's timeline. We keep you informed at every stage.
Can I buy property in Cyprus remotely? +
Yes. Many of our clients purchase Cyprus property without being present in Cyprus. We can act under a Power of Attorney to sign documents, attend the Land Registry, and manage the entire transaction on your behalf. You receive regular updates and all documentation electronically.
What are common legal risks in Cyprus property purchases? +
Common risks include purchasing property with existing mortgages or encumbrances, incomplete title deeds (particularly on older developments), illegal construction or permit issues, and contracts that do not adequately protect the buyer. Thorough due diligence by our team identifies and resolves these issues before you sign or pay any funds.
