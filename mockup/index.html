Common Questions

Residency FAQ

Can I work in Cyprus with a Permanent Residency permit? +
The standard Category F permanent residency permit does not automatically grant the right to work. However, if you establish a company in Cyprus, you can work through that company as a director. Speak to our team about combining your residency with a corporate structure.
Do I need to live in Cyprus full-time? +
No. You only need to visit Cyprus at least once every two years to maintain your permanent residency permit. This makes Cyprus ideal for investors and entrepreneurs who travel frequently.
How long does the application process take? +
From document preparation to permit issuance, the full process typically takes 6–9 months. Our team handles every step — preliminary due diligence, document preparation, Civil Registry submission, and biometric collection.
Can my adult children be included in the application? +
Minor children (under 18) can be included. Adult children (18–25) who are financially dependent students may also qualify in certain cases. Our immigration team will assess your family's specific situation.
What happens to my permit if I sell the property? +
You must maintain your qualifying investment to keep your permanent residency permit. If you sell, you must reinvest in another qualifying asset. We help you manage this transition seamlessly.

Common Questions

Corporate Law FAQ

How long does it take to form a Cyprus company? +
Typically 2–4 weeks from engagement to certificate of incorporation. This includes name reservation at the Registrar of Companies, preparation of the Memorandum & Articles of Association, director and shareholder documentation, and official filing. Rush registration (5–7 business days) is available for an additional government fee. Bank account opening adds approximately 1–2 weeks.
Can a 100% foreign-owned company be incorporated in Cyprus? +
Yes. Cyprus imposes no restrictions on foreign ownership. A Cyprus private limited company (Ltd) can be 100% owned and directed by foreign nationals, with a single shareholder and single director permitted. There is no requirement for local directors or a local physical presence, although maintaining economic substance in Cyprus is advisable for full treaty benefits.
What is the Notional Interest Deduction (NID) and how does it help? +
The NID allows Cyprus companies to deduct a notional interest expense on new equity introduced into the business, reducing taxable income by up to 80%. This is essentially a tax-free return on equity capital — calculated at the 10-year government bond yield of the country where the funds are deployed, plus 3%. Combined with the 15% rate, companies can achieve an effective tax rate as low as 3% on equity-financed income.
Does Cyprus have double tax treaties with my country? +
Cyprus has one of the most extensive double tax treaty networks in the world, with over 65 countries including Russia, Ukraine, India, China, UAE, Germany, France, the UK, the US and most EU member states. These treaties significantly reduce or eliminate withholding taxes on dividends, interest and royalties, making Cyprus an ideal holding and financing jurisdiction for international structures.
What are the ongoing compliance requirements for a Cyprus company? +
Cyprus companies must file annual returns with the Registrar of Companies, prepare and submit audited financial statements (statutory audit is mandatory for all Cyprus companies), file corporate income tax returns (IR4), and maintain VAT registrations where applicable. Our firm provides complete corporate secretarial, accounting, and compliance services to keep your company fully compliant year-round.
